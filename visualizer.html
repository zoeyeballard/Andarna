Action chunking vs single-step Core concept

Standard behavioral cloning predicts one action per timestep — errors compound fast. ACT predicts a whole chunk of 100 future actions at once, committing to a coherent plan that slashes compounding error.

Each timestep, the policy predicts just the next action. Errors compound — one bad prediction shifts the robot off the demo distribution, and it snowballs.

Diffusion Policy Iterative denoising

Unlike ACT's single forward pass, Diffusion Policy starts with pure noise and iteratively refines it into an expert-like action trajectory. More expressive for multimodal distributions, but costs N forward passes instead of one.

Expert trajectory Current prediction
ACT (1 forward pass)
Transformer decodes chunk in one shot. Fast (~5ms). Deterministic via CVAE mean.
Diffusion Policy (N steps)
Iteratively refines from noise. Slower (~N × 5ms). Captures multimodal distributions faithfully.

The INT8-vs-INT4 anomaly (reproduced)
INT8 (1365 ms) is 2.4× slower than INT4 (572 ms). bitsandbytes' LLM.int8() splits each matmul into an INT8 path plus an FP16 outlier path with per-op quant/dequant — overhead that outweighs the bandwidth saved. INT4 (NF4) uses a lighter dequant-then-matmul kernel. Neither beats native BF16 tensor-core GEMMs.

P4: Roofline analysis Why decode is the wall

The A10G's ridge point is where a kernel flips from memory-bound to compute-bound (peak compute ÷ 600 GB/s). Mapping OpenVLA's kernels onto it explains everything — the LLM decode is deeply memory-bound, which is why it dominates wall-clock and why quantizing weights is the theoretically correct lever.

The FLOPs-vs-time paradox
Decode does 2.1% of the arithmetic but 59% of the wall-clock — tensor cores idle ~99% of decode while weights stream from HBM. (Validated: analytic 3.79 TFLOP/step matched the profiler's aten::mm exactly.) This is the bottleneck that flow-matching VLAs like π0 sidestep — see below.

π0 flow matching & World Action Models The frontier, connected to P4

P4 found the wall: OpenVLA's autoregressive action decode is memory-bound and 59% of latency, capping the policy at ~2.8 Hz. The frontier's answer is to stop decoding actions token-by-token. Physical Intelligence's π0 replaces the discrete action tokenizer with a flow-matching action head that generates the whole action chunk in just 1–10 denoising steps — reaching 50 Hz. The other paradigm — World Action Models (WAMs)
Where a VLA maps image + language → action, a World Action Model is built on a pretrained video-diffusion backbone: it learns physical dynamics by predicting how the world will look next, then conditions actions on that imagined future — "pretrained to imagine, fine-tuned to act." Because the backbone already encodes physics from internet-scale video, WAMs can serve as zero-shot policies and as their own simulators / data generators. It is the convergence of the world-model line (Dreamer, Genie, NVIDIA Cosmos) with action, and the main structural alternative to the flow-matching VLA line that π0 leads. The field's arc: single-task policies (2022) → multi-task VLAs (2023) → cross-embodiment generalists like π0 (2024–25) → whole-body humanoid control and WAMs (2026).
